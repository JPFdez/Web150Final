Project Phase and Deliverables
Phase Deliverable Description
Project Initiation Project Charter This is very important document that explains the purpose, vision, objectives, scope and deliverables of the project, how the project will be structured and how it supposed to be implemented; it includes stakeholders, roles and responsibilities.
Feasibility Study In this document potential solutions are reviewed, assessed, studied, and the most feasible one is selected. This document expounds business requirements for the selected solution.
Project Planning Resource management Resource Management Plan summarizes the quality and quantity of resources required for completion the project. It exactly specifies the quantities of labor, equipment, materials etc. needed to complete your project.
Financial Plan This document identifies the money required to match project objectives.
Risk Management This document identifies risks within the project, categorizes, ranks and prioritize s each risk, including the possible impact on the project, and determining the likelihood of the risks occurring
Project Execution Quality Assurance & Procurements This Procurement process involves narrowing down a specific seller, settling on a contract, and signing an agreement. To do so, one needs to assess whether they want to make or buy, consider the criteria involved in source selection, understand the criteria of their seller, and finalize a statement of work.
Managing Stakeholders This part of the execution phase involves managing the expectations of your stakeholders, including their needs, any issues they may have and answering all questions to ensure their fundamental understanding of the process. The primary components of this process are change requests and the issue log.
